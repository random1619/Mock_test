Which Article of the Constitution of India deals with the 'Pardoning Power of the Governor'?
    Article 189
    Article 161
    Article 173    
    Article 150
Solution
As per Article 161, the Governor of a state enjoys pardoning power, where as the same power has been given to President as per Article 72. Five types of pardoning powers are-
* Pardon
* Respite
* Reprieve
* Remit
* Commute
They are enjoyed by both of them but the only difference between their powers is that the President may pardon an order of court martial and a death sentence, which such any power is not available to the governor.

How many types of writs can be issued under Articles 32 and 226 of the Indian Constitution?
    Seven
    Five
    four    
    Six
Solution
Total Five types of writs can be issued under articles 32 and 226 of the Indian constitution. The five types of writs issued in India are Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition and Quo-Warranto.

The Power of the Supreme Court of India to decide disputes between the Centre and the States falls under its
    Advisory jurisdiction
    Original jurisdiction
    Appellate jurisdiction    
    Jurisprudence
Solution
The power of the Supreme Court of India to decide disputes between the centre and the states falls under, its original jurisdiction. The original jurisdiction of a court is the power to hear a case for the first time as opposed to appellate jurisdiction when a court has the power to review a lower court's decision. In India the Supreme Court has exclusive original jurisdiction on all cases between the government and the states or between government and states on side and one or more states on other side or cases between different states. In addition, Article 32 of the Constitution of India grants writ jurisdiction to the Supreme Court on all cases involving the enforcement of Fundamental Rights of citizens of India.

To ensure a fair trial in courts the Indian Constitution has provided 3 rights. Which of the following is not one of those 3 rights?
    no person would be punished for the same offence more than once
    no person born in India will be tried under foreign laws
    no law shall declare any action as illegal from a backdate    
    no person shall be asked to give evidence against himself or herself.
Solution
The three rights provided by Constitution of India to ensure fair trial are described in Article 20 of Indian Constitution. These are–
i. No person shall be convicted of any offence except for violation of the law in force at, the time of the commission of the act charged as an offence nor be subjected to a penalty greater than that which might have been inflicted under the law in force at the time of the commission of the offence.
ii. No person shall be prosecuted and punished for the same offence more than once.
iii. No person accused of any offence shall be compelled to be a witness against himself.
