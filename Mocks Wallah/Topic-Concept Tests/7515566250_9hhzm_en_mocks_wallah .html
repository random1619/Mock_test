In 1921-22, the farmers of the Malabar coast of Kerala organized a great revolt known as the – –––.
    Kol rebellion
    Moplah rebellion
    Munda rebellion    
    Santhal rebellion
Solution
On 20 August 1921, a group of Muslim agricultural workers in Kerala's Malabar region went up in arms against "unfair land" of the British, which while granting landowners complete ownership of agricultural lands, reduced this group to mere labourers with no control over the land they tilled or the produce there of. The Malabar Rebellion or the Mapilla or Moplah rebellion started off as a peasant movement against the British and the upper-caste Hindu landlords, owing mainly to the new land-laws introduced by the British government in the early 19th century. The movement was led by the Moplahs, the indigenous Muslim Community in Kerala's Malabar region. This uprising which carried on for several months led to the death of around 10000 people.
