In chola administration,___________was the assembly in the villages which were inhabited predominantly by the Brahmanas.
    Nagaram
    Sabha
    Ur    
    Khilya
Solution
Sabha was the assembly in the villages which were inhabited predominantly by the Brahmanas in the Chola administration.
It was a local self-government institution that functioned as a council of elders who were responsible for the administration and maintenance of the village.
Sabha was also responsible for the collection of taxes, maintenance of law and order, and resolving disputes within the village.
The members of the Sabha were usually Brahmanas who were elected by the villagers.
Sabha was an important institution in the Chola administration as it helped in the smooth functioning of the village administration.

The period of influence of the Chola rulers of the South was:
    17th Century AD
    9th Century AD
    19th Century AD    
    9th Century BC
Solution
The reign of the Cholas began in the 9th century AD when they defeated the Pallavas to come into power. This rule stretched over for five long centuries until the 13th century. However, around the 2nd century, the state Andhra has a Chola kingdom that flourished far and wide. The early periods of the Chola rule saw the onset of the Sangam literature. Kantaman was one of the prominent rulers of this era. The medieval period was the era of absolute power and development for the Cholas. This is when kings like Aditya I and Parantaka I. This magnificence lasted until the arrival of the Pandyas in the early 13th century.
