Whose attack on India resulted in the Third Battle of Panipat?
    Babar
    Ahamed Shah Abdali
    Nadir Shah    
    Mohammad
Solution
The Third Battle of Panipat was fought between the Maratha Empire and the Afghan Army. It took place on 14 January 1761 at Panipat. The Battle was fought between Sadashiv Rao Bhau[The Maratha Empire] and Ahmad Shah Afdali/Durrani [The Afghan Army]. In this Battle, the Afghans were led by their king Ahmed Shah Durrani, who got help from the Rohilla Afghans of the Doab and Shuja-ud-Daula, the Nawab of Awadh. On the other hand, the Marathas failed to get the support of the Sikhs or the Jats or the Rajputs. It Is considered one of the largest and most fiercely fought battles in the 18th century.

Sher Shah Suri, a medieval ruler, focused on the construction of saris for the convenience of travelers. Who among the following initiated 'Din-illahi'?
    Babur
    Humayun
    Jahangir    
    Akbar
Solution
Din-i-Ilahi, "the religion of God," was a system of religious beliefs introduced by the great Mughal emperor Akbar in 1582 AD. His idea was to combine Islam and Hinduism into one faith, but also to add aspects of Christianity, Zoroastrianism and Jainism. It is based on Monotheism [believe in one God]. The first initiated disciples of Din-i-ilahi during emperor Akbar included Birbal, Prince Salim and Abul Fazl. Akbar took a deep personal interest in religious matters. He founded an academy, the Ibadat Khana, "the House of Worship," in 1575, where representatives of all major faiths could meet to discuss questions on theology. Listening to these debates, Akbar concluded that he should create the Din-i-ilahi.
