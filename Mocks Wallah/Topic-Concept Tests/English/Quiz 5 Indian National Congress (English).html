At which place did the Working Committee of the Indian National Congress approve the 'Quit India' resolution?

    Calcutta

    Nagpur

    Wardha

    Delhi

Solution

On 8 August 1942 at the All-India Congress Committee session in Wardha, Bombay, Gandhiji launched the Quit India movement and called for 'Do or Die'. The Wardha Resolution is also known as Quit India Resolution. The Quit India movement in 1942 shortly merged itself into the revolutionary or terrorist movement. From August 9 to August 13, there was a widespread disturbance in Bombay, Ahemadabad, Poona, and Delhi., all of them are commonly called the “Great August Uprising“. The Quit India movement, more than anything united the Indian people against British rule. The Cripps' Mission and its failure also played an important role in Gandhi's call for The Quit India Movement.
